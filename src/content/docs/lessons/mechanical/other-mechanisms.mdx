The drivetrain is one of the most important components of the robot, as it dictates the speed, maneuverability, and overall reliability of the machine. However, it is only one part of a much larger system. Every subsystem is designed with a specific purpose, and together they enable the robot to complete complex game tasks efficiently. The following sections introduce the robot's major subsystems—including the bellypan, intake, indexer, elevator, spindexer, and other key mechanisms—and explain how each contributes to the robot's overall performance.

---

## Bellypan![](Fab%20Test-1.png)

The bellypan is essentially the framing on the drivetrain (used as framing to all the drivetrain variants). The bellypan holds the electrical components including the RoboRio, PDH, Falcons battery etc. The bellypan is the primary structural plate of the robot and serves as the foundation for both the mechanical and electrical systems. It provides mounting points for the drivetrain and electrical components while ensuring the chassis remains rigid under operational loads. A well-designed bellypan optimizes weight distribution, lowers the robot's center of gravity so that it is more difficult for the robot when in use to tip over and end the math, and protects critical electronics from impacts. Additionally, it would be ideally be in a organized layout which would facillitate efficient wiring, easier troubleshooting, and faster repairs during competitions when it most matters. The final bellypan is made out of either aluminum or composite materials
