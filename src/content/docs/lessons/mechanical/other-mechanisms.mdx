The drivetrain is one of the most important components of the robot, as it dictates the speed, maneuverability, and overall reliability of the machine. However, it is only one part of a much larger system. Every subsystem is designed with a specific purpose, and together they enable the robot to complete complex game tasks efficiently. The following sections introduce the robot's major subsystems—including the bellypan, intake, indexer, elevator, spindexer, and other key mechanisms—and explain how each contributes to the robot's overall performance.

---

## Bellypan

The bellypan is essentially the framing on the drivetrain (used as framing to all the drivetrain variants). The bellypan holds the electrical components including the RoboRio, PDH, Falcons battery etc. The bellypan is the primary structural plate of the robot and serves as the foundation for both the mechanical and electrical systems. It provides mounting points for the drivetrain and electrical components while ensuring the chassis remains rigid under operational loads. A well-designed bellypan optimizes weight distribution, lowers the robot's center of gravity so that it is more difficult for the robot when in use to tip over and end the math, and protects critical electronics from impacts. Additionally, it would be ideally be in a organized layout which would facillitate efficient wiring, easier troubleshooting, and faster repairs during competitions when it most matters. The final bellypan is made out of either aluminum or composite materials.

## Intakes

The intake is responsible for collecting the FRC game object and securely into the robot. Intakes come in many different shapes and sizes and are dependent on the type of game object, what chassis your robot design is. Every type of intake has its own pros and cons for example:

* Claw
  * Lower Acquisition Zone (You have to align more and is harder to collect game object)
  * Actively Intake
* Rollers
  * Better Acquisition Zone
  * Passively Intake

The claw and roller intake system is most commonly used. Claws are usually used in games where prioritizing the amount of objects you intake at once is not a priority because it can usually only hold one game object at a time. Claw intakes were essential in FRC games like 2017 (Steamworks) and 2018 (FIRST Power Up). Because these games required manipulating specific, oddly-shaped game objects—like delicate plastic Gears in 2017 or large, heavy Cubes in 2018—claws were necessary to securely grip, lift, and place the objects without dropping them. The rollers on the other hand, were used alot in 2025 (REEFSCAPE) and 2026 (REBUILT) where efficiently collecting objects was the priority.

## Storage Systems

### Indexer

The indexer brings game pieces up to a shooting/launching mechanism. It usually contains belts or rollers to guide game objects to desired position. For example, in 2022 Rapid React the indexer was commonly used to bring cargo up to a shooter or turret. It is an essential component of the robot because it largely dictates how many game objects you can dispense/shoot or how fast and efficient a game object is placed into the desired position.

### Spindexer

The spindexer serves a similar function to the indexer where it prepares to feed it into the indexer. It is usually used in games involving balls and spherical objects due to the spindexer's shape.&#x20;
