Welcome to the mechanical subteam!

Within the FRC work environment, the mechanical subteam is responsible for assembling and fabricating the robot from CAD models. Most of the work is done in the workshop where members will learn to use a plethora of hand tools and power tools. Being a part of the mechanical subteam is not just about fabrication, but it is also important for members to understand the design and prototype with the design team. This way members are aware of the decisions made with design and are able to make iterations when fabricating and assembling.

Throughout the season, the team iterates on designs based on testing results, ensuring each mechanism is reliable, lightweight, and easy to service during competitions. During competitions, some mechanical members will be assigned to help in the pit with repairs.

---

## What you'll learn

- Shop safety (always lesson one)
- Hand tools and power tools
- Drivetrain fundamentals
- Prototyping with the design team

As we continue, mechanical members will be expected to understand the design fundamentals and components of conventional robotics mechanisms. This includes learning how different mechanical systems work, why certain design decisions are made, and how to identify areas for improvement. Members will gradually transition from simply assembling parts to contributing ideas during brainstorming, prototyping, and design reviews.

Throughout training, you'll gain experience with common FRC hardware such as bearings, shafts, gears, sprockets, belts, pulleys, chain, fasteners, and structural components. Understanding FRC components are important to build familiarity and effectiveness when working in the FRC workspace. You'll also learn proper measuring techniques, manufacturing practices, and how to interpret CAD models, fabrication sheets and engineering drawings to accurately fabricate parts.

---

## Mandatory Safety Training

In the FRC workspace there are many potential dangerous hazards; thus, members must undergo training to understand safety in the lab. Simultaneously, the robotics sponsors are responsible for students so we try our best not to stress them out and maintain proper safety protocols.

> **When Mechanical members first join the program, in order to work with the power tools in the robotics workshop one must finish a mandatory in-person safety training and a fabrication test.**

The **safety training** will be conducted by a trusted mechanical member or leader. It will cover all the power tools and general usages of the tools in the workshop. This is a great opportunity to ask questions and familiarize yourself with the environment. This training ensures proper usage of the machines and proper safety protocols (This training will last max. 1 hr).

The **Fabrication Test** will last several days and requires mechanical members to fabricate a piece of aluminum stock. This simulates how a piece of aluminum rectangular tubing (stock) will be fabricated during the regular season.

---

One of the most valuable skills you'll develop is problem-solving. Rarely does a mechanism work perfectly on the first attempt. Mechanical members are constantly testing, troubleshooting, and refining designs to improve performance, reliability, and ease of maintenance. Learning to iterate quickly while maintaining high build quality is a fundamental part of the engineering design process.

By the end of the training program, members should be able to safely operate workshop equipment, manufacture and assemble robot components, understand the purpose of major robot mechanisms, collaborate effectively with the design subteam, and contribute confidently during both the build season and competitions.
